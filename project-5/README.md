
# Power BI Dashboard of US Accidents: 2016 - 2020

## Project Overview

This project analyzed US traffic accident data from 2016 to 2020 using Power BI to identify trends, severity, and contributing factors such as weather conditions. The dashboard provides insights on accident frequency by time and severity to inform government agencies and the public. It highlights critical patterns to aid in reducing traffic-related injuries and fatalities.

---

## Business Problem

Road traffic crashes are a leading cause of death in the US for ages 1–54, with millions injured and over 90 daily fatalities. Despite fewer vehicles during COVID-19, fatal accidents increased due to reckless driving. There is a need for detailed analysis to understand accident trends and causes to improve road safety.

---

## Objective

- Analyze accident frequency and severity trends across all 50 states
- Identify temporal patterns for safer travel times by month, day, and hour
- Assess impact of weather conditions on accident occurrence and severity

---

## Tools & Technologies

- Power BI
- DAX
- Data Grouping and Binning
- ETL processes
- National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) data

---

## Project Workflow

- Gather and preprocess US accident data from NHTSA
- Group weather conditions into meaningful categories
- Create visualizations including line and clustered column charts
- Analyze accidents per day, severity, and year-over-year changes
- Develop and format an interactive Power BI dashboard for insights

---

## Key Insights

- Fatal accident percentages increased during COVID-19 despite fewer vehicles on roads
- Reckless driving contributed significantly to the rise in fatal accidents
- Certain weather conditions correlate with higher accident frequencies
- Best travel times vary by month, day, and hour to minimize accident risk
