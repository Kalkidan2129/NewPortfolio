
# Power BI Dashboard of US Accidents: 2016 - 2020

## Project Overview

This project created an interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing US traffic accident data from 2016 to 2020 across all 50 states. It highlights trends in accident frequency, severity, timing, and contributing factors such as weather conditions. The dashboard provides actionable insights to inform government agencies and the public on reducing traffic fatalities and injuries.

---

## Business Problem

Road traffic crashes are a leading cause of death in the US, especially for ages 1–54, with millions injured and thousands killed annually. Despite fewer vehicles on roads during COVID-19, fatal accident rates have increased due to reckless driving. There is a critical need for comprehensive data analysis to understand accident trends and support effective safety interventions.

---

## Objective

- Analyze accident frequency and severity trends year-over-year across all states
- Identify temporal patterns to determine safest travel times by month, day, and hour
- Assess impact of weather conditions on accident rates to inform preventive measures

---

## Tools & Technologies

- Power BI
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- ETL processes
- Data visualization
- Data mining
- National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) datasets

---

## Project Workflow

- Extract and clean US accident data from NHTSA for 2016-2020
- Transform data to calculate KPIs such as YOY accidents and severity percentages
- Develop Power BI dashboard with cards and formatted data labels for key metrics
- Visualize accident trends by time, severity, and weather conditions
- Publish and share dashboard to inform stakeholders

---

## Key Insights

- Fatal accident percentages increased during the COVID-19 era despite fewer vehicles on roads
- Reckless driving contributed significantly to the rise in fatal accidents
- Certain months, days, and hours show higher accident frequencies indicating safer travel windows
- Weather conditions are a notable factor influencing accident severity nationwide
