
# Conflict in Ethiopia (1997 - 2015)

## Project Overview

This project analyzed conflict data in Ethiopia from 1997 to 2015, focusing on ethnic tensions and resource disputes. Using Power BI, it visualized deaths and conflict trends by interaction groups to uncover underlying causes. The analysis highlighted the role of land disputes and federal ethnic structures in driving conflicts.

---

## Business Problem

Ethiopia has experienced prolonged ethnic conflicts primarily due to disputes over land, resources, and political power. These conflicts result in casualties, displacement, and regional instability, complicating governance and development efforts. Understanding the patterns and causes is critical for informed policy-making and conflict resolution.

---

## Objective

- Visualize conflict trends and death rates by interaction groups over time
- Identify major causes and geographic patterns of ethnic conflicts
- Provide insights to support government and policy interventions

---

## Tools & Technologies

- Power BI
- Data Visualization
- Human Rights Council Conflict Data
- Line and Stacked Column Charts
- Data Filtering and Interaction Features

---

## Project Workflow

- Collect and preprocess conflict data from Human Rights Council records
- Design Power BI visuals including line and stacked column charts
- Configure filters and interaction groups for detailed analysis
- Analyze death rates and year-over-year/month-over-month trends
- Generate insights and summarize findings for policy relevance

---

## Key Insights

- Majority of conflicts stem from land and resource disputes among ethnic groups
- Federal structure based on ethnicity exacerbates tensions and disparities
- Conflicts lead to significant casualties and displacement of citizens
- Visual trends reveal fluctuating death rates linked to interaction groups
